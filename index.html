Education

I received a PhD in Mathematics from The Ohio State University in 2018. The title of my thesis was "Properties of SU(2, 1) Hecke-Maas cusp forms and Eisenstein series." My advisor was Roman Holowinsky. I also helped out toward the beginning of his Erdős institute both as a student and after.

During my doctorate, I had the opportunity to spend a semester at the Université de Bordeaux working with Guillaume Martin. I really enjoyed being in France.

I also have a master's degree from the Program in Applied and Computational Mathematics from Princeton, as well as an undergraduate degree in mathematics from Pomona College.

During my mathematical studies I became in tune with canonical web design from studying math professors' websites. Consider that of Abel Prize winner Michel Talagrand.
